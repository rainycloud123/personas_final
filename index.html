My Interface: Vending Machine

Interview Questions:

    Expectations:
  • What are you thinking about when using a vending machine?
  • Do you find vending machines convenient? Why?
  • If you had the choice between getting the same snickers from a vending machine vs from a cashier which would you choose? Why?
  • What do you think are the benefits of using a vending machine?
  • What annoys you about vending machines?
  • Do you always expect the same process when using a vending machine?
  • Do you think a person of any age could successfully use a vending machine?
  • What challenges might arise if someone is visually impaired?
  • Are some vending machines better than others and if so what makes some better than others?
    • Experience:
  • Have you ever been frustrated with this interface and why?
  • Are you usually pleased or displeased with this interface?
  • Are your expectations met when using a vending machine?
  • How would you describe the efficiency of this product?
    • Motives:
  • Do you find this more of a necessity or a pleasure?
  • Do you look forward to this activity?
    • Challenges:
  • Have you ever had a problem with this interface?
  • What would you change about a vending machine if anything

Summary:

  • Users used vending machines when desperate for quick and convenient snacks (so often in a hurry), not for pleasure. They go into the process expecting smooth transactions and easy use.
  • User expressed benefits of vending machine use include getting a quick snack, avoiding human interaction and avoiding human error on the seller side.
  • User expressed drawbacks include price, lack of choices/unhealthy choices, machine failure (especially important), difficulty paying (related to machine function), snacks getting stuck (related to machine function), and placement of machines targeting those who will fall for drawbacks (such as schools and workplaces with limited other food options).
  • Userâ€™s main frustrations included lack of interface accessibility, when payment doesn't work (malfunction), when a snack gets stuck (malfunction), and when the machine is not well kept so snacks inside appear older.
  • Users felt that machines could be improved with more food choice and price decreases but also as it relates to the interface itself, machines could be improved with increased accessibility, reliability, and a more straightforward/consistent order of performing tasks across all vending machines.

Problem: Trying to get snacks to people conveniently and quickly without the need of a human/in-person vendor. Key components: Keypad: where you can type the number of associated snacks and either select it or find out its price. Payment box: where you can swipe/tap your card. (also have a cash insert in a more detailed sketch). Screen (with check mark): Shows current state of transaction. Pickup compartment: Where you push in to get your snack which is designed in such a way that you cannot reach your hand up to steal the snack.
